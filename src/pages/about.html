Timken

IT Analyst :: June 2023 - November 2023 :: Duncan, SC

It is with absolute certainty that I can say this has been my favorite job in my career thus far. The job itself was an entertaining challenge, the people I worked with were mostly very personable and easy to get along with, and there were no massive red flags that took place during my time. Given the opportunity and the right location, I would work for Timken again. It's not perfect, but it's given me the least trouble.

  • Responded to, worked on, and resolved problem tickets as they came in: printer issues and toner refills, computer software and hardware problems, user accounts, software and hardware installation, and label printer repair. Issues with printers that could not immediately be fixed or were otherwise outside of the scope of IT (broken or malfunctioning parts, special access required) were forwarded to a third party.
  • Replaced and updated warehouse shop floor machines Windows OS to company standard. Machines that could not be updated or were otherwise problematic were replaced with newly imaged machines.
  • Repaired and replaced label printers as needed; warehouse had a surplus of label printers, so any needed parts for the printers could be pulled from decommissioned ones with minimal wait time. Adjusted print hardware to produce quality prints.
  • Created and deployed internal page for auditing packed orders using HTML, CSS, JS, and Classic ASP, and T-SQL. Page was made to more display and more accurately capture issues with audited orders and scale to 8-inch tablet screens.
  • Prepared and deployed Android-based warehouse scanners to company specifications. Scanners setup with Android Enterprise required collaboration with overseas team to download and install software and network certificates for the internal network. Those scanners not deployed sit in storage, ready to deploy or replace at any time.
